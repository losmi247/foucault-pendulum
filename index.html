Foucault pendulum

General Information

A Foucault pendulum consists of a mass \(m\) hanging from a cable of length \(l\), which is hooked to the ceiling in such a way that it allows the mass to move freely in any possible direction.

The frequency of oscillation of such a pendulum is, as usual, \( w_0 = \sqrt{\frac{g}{l}} \). However, due to the planet Earth's rotation around its axis at angular speed \( \omega_E \approx \frac{2\pi}{24h} \approx 7 \cdot 10^{-5} \frac{rad}{s} \), the plane in which the hanging mass oscillates is constantly rotating. The French physicist LÃ©on Foucault proposed that the period of rotation of the plane of oscillation is related to the sine of the longitude of its location \(q\) in the following way: $$ T = \frac{24h}{\sin q}$$

It can be shown that the pendulum's complicated motion is, irrespective of the mass \(m\), described by the following equation:

$$ u(t) = C_1 e^{\gamma_1 t} + C_2 e^{\gamma_2 t} $$

where \(u(t)\) is a complex number representing the current position of the mass, \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) are complex constants, and complex numbers \(\gamma_{1,2}\) are denoted by the following expression (we use \(\Omega = \omega_E \sin \phi\), where \(\phi\) is the latitude of the pendulums location):

$$ \gamma_{1,2} = i(-\Omega \pm \sqrt{\Omega^2 + w_0^2}) $$
